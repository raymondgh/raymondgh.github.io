Challenge

In 2011 and 2012, Microsoft's new design language for Windows 8 (formerly known as Metro UI) presented an exciting platform for new developers to access a wide market of users. To seize the opportunity, we needed to be first movers. What could we build?

Solution

World Flags 2.0 is an educational game in flashcard format that effectively teaches users the national flags of the world. The inspiration came from a game I once played to study for a high school French exam. We developed World Flags 2.0 as a single page application, which was a style still gaining popularity at the time. We were successfully able to launch to English audiences within a few weeks of Windows 8's general availability. As time went on and our downloads climbed, we localized the game in 8 other languages, boosting our popularity even further. After 10 months of accelerating growth and top category rankings in a few markets, we decided to test monetization with a price point of $1.49. Two months and $22.38 in revenue later, we opted not to renew our Windows 8 developer license and the app was removed from the store. Fun trivia: we added the "2.0" to the title of our app because someone had already reserved the simple name "World Flags." A great memory from this project is a negative review from a Chinese user who insisted that we update the game to exclude Taiwan as a country.
